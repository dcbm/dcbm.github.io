Do some VDA with sights of Vienna, Austria.

So what should I visit when staying in Vienna?

I heard this question quite often when friends were visiting from different countries. My Answer was always, if you are only one or two days in Vienna, just talk a long walk in the city center and enjoy the flare of the city, since the most pretty buildings are around the city center and mostly in walking distance. This and Schönbrunn, because, I mean Schönbrunn!

Although this are easily two days of full program to spend in Vienna (when you count at least one or two stops at restaurants or in one of the many coffee places), I wanted to start with an data-driven approach to find optimal “visiting-scenarios” for tourists.

Limitations

  1. Every sight should be in walking distance.
  2. Public transportation in Vienna is great, but for now we not look at close station
  3. We focus on staying outside, museums might be on the list, but you can spend easily 4h in one of them, thus only getting impressed by the nice architecture.

Data

The data comes from publicly available data, found on: https://data.wien.gv.at.

Time to make routes.

Lets remove the map and focus on the coordinates

According to my hypothesis there are two clusters of sights in Vienna. Lets check out the blue sqares first.

Schönbrunn tour

According to the limitations you can spend an amazing day in one of the most famous castles and parks of Vienna. Some remarks of this tour:

  1. The Tiergarten Schönbrunn has not only pandas, but a visit in one of the oldest zoos in the world can also take more than 4h if you are really into animals and want to see every of this little (or huge) fellows. Same counts for the ‘Technisches Museum’- Vienna Technical Museum (except the animals).
  2. From the Gloriette you have a great view over vienna, since it is built a bit uphill and has great cakes too.
  3. Visit the Palmenhaus if you like plants otherwise get impressed by its architecture.
  4. If you like Maria Theresia and Sissy you will also enjoy a visit in the castle itself.

The big one in the middle

The green triangles are a bit more tricky, and needs a bit more “domain-knowledge”, lets break it apart:

my standard tour

Here we see the tour that i usually suggest if people visit Vienna for the first time.

The route includes big parts of the city center and its sights. Some important notes:

  1. With Stephansdom & Karlskirche we include two of the most impressive churches.
  2. The Anker Uhr is a secret highlight, since it is not well known and is worth a visit everyday at 12 o’clock
  3. There are many museums on the track: Naturhistorisches Museum, Kunsthistorisches Museum, Wien Museum, Albertina, and Sisi Museum, again, you can spend hours in there.

the not so standard ring tour

This is the second half of the so called ‘ring’ around the city center of Vienna.

Here we include a visit to the city hall, the houses of parlament, the MuseumsQuartier, with its many different museums, and my favorite church of Vienna, the Votivkirche. If you start the tour at the Votivkirche, you can finish it with a visit at Vienna’s Aquarium which is inside an impressive Tower. The walk there might seem a bit much, but the Tower is located close to Viennas longest shopping street.

Belvedere

A smaller Tour, if you start from Karlskirche, walk pass the Schwarzenbergplatz, with its impressive fountain, you can reach another impressive viennese castle: The Belvedere, here you can visit its garden as well as enjoy Klimt’s famous painting: the Kiss.
